Effects of Telecom Customer Attributes on Churn and Their LTV¶

by Andrew Shannon¶

Investigation Overview¶

In this investigation, I wanted to dive into the various attributes of the Kaggle Telco Customer Churn data set and identify variables that seemed to correlate with churn.

Dataset Overview¶

The Telco data set consists of 7032 records with 21 different variables. The variables consist of monthly charges, total charges, customer tenure, and a variety of other categorical attributes used to describe the cuustomers. I dropped customers that had 0 total charges because they also have 0 for tenure.

Distribution of Customer Tenure¶

The distribution of customer tenure is bimodal. However, it is very interesting in that the first peak is for new customers while the second peak is for long term customers.

Distribution of Customer Total Charges¶

As seen in the distribution of customer tenures, there is a peak of new and old customers. That lead to a skew in the total charges distribution that required a logarithmic transformation. Post transformation, we can see that there is another multimodal distribution - Small peaks under \$100 and then another peak around \\$1500, and between \$4-6k.

Customer Survival Rate¶

When we view the surivival function for the entire population, it is estimated that 15% of customers will churn within the first year, and reaching 40% by year 6.

## Create an estimate
kmf.plot()
plt.title('Telco Customer Survival Rate')
plt.ylabel('Survival Rate')

plt.xticks(ticks=[12, 24, 36, 48, 60, 72]);

Customer Survival Rate by Contract Type¶

However, accross the different customer segments this varies greatly, and is best represented between the different contract durations. As expected, two year contracts have the best survival rate followed by one year. 30% of month to month subscribers tend to churn within the first year with 90% churning by year 6.

Customer Survival Rate by Monthly Bin¶

Low bill customers have the highest survival rate as after 6 years roughly 80% remain customers. Meanwhile, customers with high bills have the lowest survival rate. 20% of this segment churn within the first year with 50% churning within 6 years.

Customer Survival Rate by Internet Service¶

Customers without internet service have the highest survival rate as 90% remain after 6 years. Fiber subscribers turn out to be the customers with the worst survival rate. 20% churn within the first year with nearly 60% churning within 6 years.

The Shortest and Longest Tenured Segments¶

What is interesting - when we begin to segment the customers farther, we are able to identfy which attributes lead to shorter tenures, and which paths lead to longer tenures. On average, customers with two year contracts, very high bills, phone service, and fiber as having the longest tenure. On the other hand - Month to month, low bills, phone service, and DSL internet have the lowest tenure.

The Shortest and Longest Tenured Segment Survival¶

Looking at the surivival rates for these two segments, we can see that within 2 years 60% of the shortest tenured segment have churned while as we reach 72 months, only 10% of the longest tenure group has churned.

T = df.tenure
E = df.encoded_churn

contract = df['Contract']
bins = df['monthly_bins']
